\begin{abstract}
	In a companion paper we showed that stereographic encoding
	lifts the boundedness constraint of quantum signal processing
	(QSP) for single-qubit signals, producing unbounded rational
	eigenvalue transformations via a classical change of the
	encoding/decoding interface.  Here we develop the full
	multi-qubit extension.  We define stereographic block
	encoding---an exact, single-ancilla embedding that places each
	eigenvalue of a Hermitian operator onto the Bloch sphere
	without normalization---and show that the QSP protocol lifts to
	a multi-qubit eigenvalue transformation acting independently in
	each eigenspace.  The construction requires knowledge of the
	eigenbasis, restricting applicability to structured
	Hamiltonians; we give explicit circuits for diagonal operators,
	Pauli-$Z$ sums, and the Heisenberg model, analyzing gate counts
	and comparing with standard block encoding.  We characterize
	the decoding protocol when the system register is in a
	superposition of eigenspaces, quantify the
	$\mathcal{O}(r^6/\epsilon^2)$ measurement overhead, and present
	a head-to-head comparison with standard quantum singular value
	transformation (QSVT): circuit depth is reduced by up to
	$10^4\times$ at condition number $\kappa = 100$, while the
	total cost (depth $\times$ shots) favors standard QSVT due to
	measurement overhead.  The results establish a
	depth-versus-shots tradeoff that is favorable on
	coherence-limited hardware processing structured operators.
\end{abstract}
