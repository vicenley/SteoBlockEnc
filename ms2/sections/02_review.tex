\section{Review of Stereographic QSP}
\label{sec:review}

This section summarizes the single-qubit stereographic QSP
framework established in the companion
paper~\cite{KharaziLeytonOrtega2026a}.  The material is presented
without proofs (which can be found in that reference) but in
sufficient detail to make the present paper self-contained.

\subsection{Stereographic Encoding}
\label{subsec:review-encoding}

For $z \in \C$, the stereographically encoded quantum state is
\begin{equation}
	\ket{z} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{|z|^2 + 1}}\big(z\ket{0} + \ket{1}\big).
	\label{eq:encoding}
\end{equation}
In polar form $z = re^{i\theta}$ with $r \geq 0$, the encoding
maps $\C \cup \{\infty\}$ bijectively to the Bloch sphere:
$z = 0$ maps to $\ket{1}$ (south pole), $z \to \infty$ to
$\ket{0}$ (north pole), and $|z| = 1$ traces the equator.
The Bloch vector of $\ket{z}$ is exactly the stereographic
image of~$z$.  The state is prepared from $\ket{0}$ by the
encoding unitary
\begin{equation}
	U_z = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+r^2}}\!\begin{pmatrix}
		re^{i\theta} & 1 \\ 1 & -re^{-i\theta}
	\end{pmatrix} \in \U(2),
	\label{eq:encoding-unitary}
\end{equation}
and the encoded value is recovered from the output state
$\alpha\ket{0} + \beta\ket{1}$ via the amplitude ratio
\begin{equation}
	z = \frac{\langle\PauliX\rangle + i\langle\PauliY\rangle}
	{1 - \langle\PauliZ\rangle} = \frac{\alpha}{\beta}.
	\label{eq:decoding}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Signal Operator and Basis Change}
\label{subsec:review-signal}

The stereographic signal operator
$S_z \equiv U_z\,\PauliZ$ encodes the unbounded parameter
$r$ into a unitary gate.  For real signals ($\theta = 0$), it
reduces to a rotation $R(\phi)$ with $\phi = \arccos(\rtilde)$
and $\rtilde = r/\sqrt{1+r^2} \in [0,1)$.  The key structural
result is the basis change identity
\begin{equation}
	S_z = V^{-1}\,W(\rtilde)\,V, \qquad V = \mathrm{diag}(1,i),
	\label{eq:basis-change}
\end{equation}
where $W(\rtilde)$ is the standard QSP signal unitary.  This
conjugation extends to the full QSP product:
\begin{equation}
	W_{\bm{\phi}}^{(\mathrm{stereo})}(r)
	= V^{-1}\,U_{\bm{\phi}}^{(\mathrm{std})}(\rtilde)\,V,
	\label{eq:full-basis-change}
\end{equation}
so that stereo-QSP is standard QSP in a rotated basis.

\subsection{Function Class and Unnormalized Formulation}
\label{subsec:review-function-class}

The output state of a degree-$d$ stereographic QSP sequence is
\begin{equation}
	W_{\bm{\phi}}\ket{0} = P(\rtilde)\ket{0} + Q(\rtilde)\ket{1},
	\label{eq:qsp-state}
\end{equation}
with $|P(\rtilde)|^2 + |Q(\rtilde)|^2 = 1$.  Decoding yields
the rational function $f(r) = P(\rtilde)/Q(\rtilde)$, which can
be unbounded.  The unnormalized signal operator
\begin{equation}
	O_r = \sqrt{1+r^2}\,S_z
	= \begin{pmatrix} r & -1 \\ 1 & r \end{pmatrix}
	\label{eq:unnormalized-signal}
\end{equation}
has polynomial entries in~$r$, and the unnormalized QSP product
satisfies $|\tilde{P}(r)|^2 + |\tilde{Q}(r)|^2 = (1+r^2)^d$,
where $\tilde{P}$ and $\tilde{Q}$ are polynomials in~$r$ with
$\deg(\tilde{P}) \leq d$ and $\deg(\tilde{Q}) \leq d-1$.
